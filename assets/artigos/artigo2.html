Investing for Beginners: Where to Start?

May 5, 2023 • 10 min read

A complete guide to take your first steps into the world of investing...

Starting to invest may seem scary, but it's simpler than you think. With the right information and some discipline, anyone can make their money work — even with small amounts. This guide will show you exactly how to start.

Why start investing early?

Investing young brings advantages you can't get back later:

  • Compound interest: Your money earns on top of earnings
  • Time to recover losses: You can take more risks
  • Healthy financial habits: You learn long-term thinking
  • Early independence: You can retire earlier
Important Fact

If you invest R$200/month starting at age 20 (at 0.7% monthly return), by age 60 you’ll have about R$600,000. If you start at 30, you’ll have around R$250,000. Time is your greatest ally!

First steps before investing

1. Organize your personal finances

Before investing, you should:

  • Track expenses and know where your money goes
  • Have an emergency fund (3–6 months of costs)
  • Be free of high-interest debts

2. Define your goals

Investments should have a purpose:

  • Short-term (1–3 years): Travel, course, car down payment
  • Medium-term (3–10 years): Home, graduate school
  • Long-term (10+ years): Retirement, financial independence

1. Treasury Direct (Selic)

The safest investment in Brazil, ideal for emergency funds:

  • Earns more than savings
  • Can be redeemed anytime
  • Minimum investment of R$30
  • Tax-free for up to R$20,000/month

2. Digital Bank CDBs

Bank Deposit Certificates with good returns:

  • Yield 100–110% of CDI
  • Some with daily liquidity
  • Protected by FGC (up to R$250,000)
Important Tip

Start with low-risk applications (Treasury Selic, CDBs) until you build your knowledge and reserve. Then you can diversify into investments with higher return potential.

How to start investing in practice

Step 1: Choose a broker

Recommended platforms for beginners:

  • Nubank (NuInvest)
  • Rico
  • XP
  • Banco Inter

All offer free accounts and simple interfaces.

Step 2: Transfer a small amount

Start with R$50–R$100 to get familiar with the platform.

Step 3: Make your first investment

Suggested first application:

  1. Go to the "Treasury Direct" section
  2. Select "Treasury Selic"
  3. Enter the amount (R$50+)
  4. Confirm the transaction

Common beginner mistakes

Avoid these traps:

  • Investing without an emergency fund: You may need to redeem at the wrong time
  • Chasing quick returns: Often involves high risk
  • Not diversifying: Putting everything in one investment
  • Ignoring fees: Some funds charge fees that eat your earnings
  • Following fads: Crypto and stocks without analysis

Next steps

After mastering basic investments, you can move on to:

  1. Real Estate Funds (REITs)
  2. Stocks of solid companies
  3. Index ETFs (like IVVB11)
  4. Private pension plans (PGBL/VGBL)

Conclusion

Investing is not just for the rich. Anyone can start with a small amount and grow over time. The key is to start early, stay consistent, and keep learning.
